Stay Safe Online-Prevention Checklist for College Students in India

General Online Safety:
1.	Use strong, unique passwords for every account. Use a password manager (e.g., Bitwarden, Google Password Manager) instead of reusing passwords.
2.	Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts — email, social media, banking apps. Prefer authenticator apps over SMS OTP where possible.
3.	Never click on unknown links received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email — even if they appear to come from known sources like banks or IRCTC. 
4.	Verify before you trust: If someone calls claiming to be from your bank or any government body, hang up and call back on the official number listed on the bank's website. 
5.	Keep your apps and OS updated — security patches fix vulnerabilities that fraudsters exploit. 
6.	Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities (banking, logging into email). Use a VPN if public Wi-Fi is necessary.

Financial/UPI Safety:
1.	Never share your UPI PIN with anyone — not even bank staff, Google Pay support, or "government officials." No legitimate entity will ever ask for it. 
2.	Understand the difference between Send and Receive: You do NOT need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to "accept" a payment, it is a SCAM — you will be sending money, not receiving it. 
3.	Check the collect request carefully before approving any UPI collect/debit request. Confirm the requester's name and amount before entering your PIN. 
4.	Do not install screen-sharing apps (AnyDesk, TeamViewer, Quick Support) at anyone's request — fraudsters use these to capture your banking screen in real time.
