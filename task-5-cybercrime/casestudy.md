What is UPI Payment Fraud?  
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of financial cybercrime where attackers deceive victims into authorizing fraudulent transactions or stealing their UPI credentials. Unlike traditional bank fraud, UPI fraud exploits the speed and ease of digital payments, often leaving victims with little time to react.  

How it typically happens?
1. Initial Contact: The fraudster contacts the victim via phone, SMS, or WhatsApp, posing as a bank representative, customer care agent, or even a buyer on an OLX/Quikr listing. 
2. Creating Urgency: They claim the victim's account is blocked, a KYC update is pending, or they want to "send" money for a product purchase. 
3. Sending a Collect Request: Instead of sending money, the attacker sends a collect/debit request through UPI apps like GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm — tricking victims into believing they need to "approve" an incoming payment. 
4. Credential Theft: In some cases, the attacker shares a screen-sharing link (e.g., AnyDesk) or a fake UPI PIN reset page to steal the victim's credentials. 
5. Transaction Executed: The victim approves the request or enters their UPI PIN, and money is instantly debited. 
6. Disappearance: The fraudster becomes unreachable immediately after.

Who is Usually Targeted?
1.	First-time digital payment users, especially in semi-urban and rural areas.
2.	College students and young adults selling items on second-hand platforms. 
3.	Senior citizens unfamiliar with UPI workflows. 
4.	Small business owners expecting incoming payments.

Consequences:
1.	Financial loss: Victims lose money instantly, with limited reversal options due to UPI’s real-time settlement.
2.	Psychological impact: Anxiety, loss of trust in digital payments.
3.	Legal complications: Filing complaints is time-consuming; recovery rates are low.
4.	Data exposure: Victims may also have their phone numbers and UPI IDs sold on the dark web.
